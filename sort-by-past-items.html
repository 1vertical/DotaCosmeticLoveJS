Introduction

A Short Story

Greetings folks! I'm back. I've made a new "analysis" of Dota 2's wardrobe. What has changed since the last... *checks publish date of last analysis* ...7 years?! Wat.

For those that missed my article on analysing which heroes received more cosmetic love than others, this is the new version! Its goal is to check who gets more love now than before. Some heroes will be rather depressing to see, they exist so long and are forgotten almost completely.

Make sure to read the notes on what was added, changed and removed from the original analysis. It also contains interesting and required knowledge to avoid bias.

Anyway, without further wasting time. Here you go!

Table Design Changes

Added

  • Added new columns. (See Notes to see what they are and what they represent.)

Changed

  • Reordered columns to make comparisons easier.
  • Data source changed from gamepedia.com to fandom.com (former URL reroutes to the fandom.com URL).

Removed

  • "Broken down?" column is removed. All heroes are currently released. For the uninitiated, this column tracked which heroes were ready/partially ready/not ready for getting new equipment.
  • Check the past publication for the list broken down status of all the heroes.

Notes

Column Explanations

  • Hero - The hero in question.
  • Slots - The total amount of slots for the character.
  • Past Sets - The past total of published sets.
  • Cur. Sets - The current total of published sets.
  • Diff. Sets - The difference between past sets and current sets.
  • Past Items - The past total of published items.
  • Cur. Items - The current total of published items.
  • Diff. Items - The difference between past total items and current total items.
  • Average - As suggested by u/FakeTherapist - The approximate average items per number of slots is included to minimize bias and display inflation ratio. Formula: Total Items/Slots. Rounded upwards.
  • Arcanas - The hero has arcanas or not.
  • Personas - The hero has personas or not.
  • Taunts - The hero has taunt animations or not.
  • Kin. Gems - The hero has kinetic gems or not.
  • Voices - The hero has voices or not.
  • Released - The release date of the hero. (In the Dota 2 client.)

Totals are Variable

  • The totals here is an approximation - I count by hand and don't web crawl the source site and it is very possible that items were added after I was done counting.

Variable Items per Set

  • Sets don't necessarily include items made for all slots. Examples: Single items, Arcanas, early lifetime sets - typically a set with 3 items instead of an item for all slots, etc.

Slots vs. Total Inflation

  • Each hero has their own amount of slots ~ this can inflate/deflate the ratio leading towards a biased total of items. The Average Factor column is added for an inflation factor for sets.

Inconsistent Slot Totals

  • Some heroes' (like tinker for example) slots differ from the website source total and workshop requirements. The maximum number is used.

Updated Slots Column

  • The Slots column was updated to the current slot counts. You will notice that some numbers are different compared to the older analysis. This can be for one or a few reasons:
    • I've perhaps made a typo or combined taunts with items in the past for some heroes.
    • The heroes were broken down further.
    • the heroes that was not broken down (but received items) was given slots.

Weapon Packs

  • Some heroes have weapon packs.
  • Weapon packs are considered as Sets and is added in the Set column for heroes that have them.

Arcanas

  • Some arcanas are either a set of items or a single item.

Duplicate and Gold Items

  • Some heroes have duplicates of the same item (like a gold version of an item for example).
  • Not all heroes have duplicates though which also serves as a factor of inflation.
  • Gold Items are considered separate items from their standard counterparts.
  • Gold Items are part of the total count.
